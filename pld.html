■ Updated: 5:40 PM 1/17/2022

Paladin is a tank with a simple linear rotation that alternates between physical melee and magical ranged phases. With interesting utility in skills like Clemency and Cover, plus the fun of using Hallowed Ground to take zero damage even during some of the hardest hitting attacks, paladin is one of the easiest tanks to pick up but can still take a lot of practice to master!

■ Openers

Paladin has two two useful opener variations. The first starts your rotation off with the Fight or Flight window, getting as many cooldowns off within raid buffs as possible and setting you right on your standard rotation loop. The second one jumps into your Requiscat window which can help align your ranged attacks with times that you need to disengage from the boss. The Fight or Flight opener is recommended in most cases.

As of writing we are still very early on in the expansion. As such openers can and will change slightly over time due to more refined testing as well as changing GCD speeds that align better with current raid tier mechanics.

Fight or Flight First

Precast Holy Spirit at 2s.

We use a precasted Holy Spirit to pull the boss and go immediately into our standard rotation loop with our Fight or Flight phase. The potion weaved after Riot Blade will last until Confiteor is cast. As always, ensure that you're fitting both Goring Blades under Fight or Flight.

Requiescat First

Precast Fight or Flight at 16 or 19 seconds, Tincture of Strength at 4s, and Holy Spirit at 2s.

Despite it's name the Requiscat first opener still uses Fight or Flight at the beginning, this time prepull to allow it to get that first Goring Blade buffed but still be off cooldown for when it's time for your next Fight or Flight phase.

Wether you want to precast Fight or Flight at 16 or 19 seconds depends on which rotation loop you'll be using. If you want the full 3 Atonements for the 64s, precast at 16s. For the 2 Atonement, 61s loop, precast at 19s. These timings have Fight or Flight come off cooldown right as it's needed.

Both openers are followed immediately by the Unbuffed phase of your rotation. Check the Rotation section for more information on the difference between the 61s and 64s loops.

■ Rotation

The paladin rotation is a minute long loop with 3 "phases" that you cycle around. First the Fight or Fight phase where you aim to get as many of your GCD and oGCD skills fit within the Fight or Flight buff as possible. Then the Requiescat phase where you expend all your Requiescat stacks, finishing with your powerful Confiteor combo. Lastly you have an "Unbuffed" phase where you bide you time with a Royal Authority combo and Atonements until Fight or Flight is off cooldown allowing you to start the loop over again.

This full loop can come out to ~64s for a standard loop, or ~61s for a shorter loop that fits better into buff windows. This is discussed more in the Unbuffed Phase section.

Fight or Flight Phase

In this phase your goal is to fit as many of your damaging oGCD abilities into the Fight or Flight buff window as possible. Following one of the standard openers this just means using those abilities as they come off cooldown as soon as possible and making sure to hold both Intervene stacks for this window as well when fight mechanics do not require the extra movement.

Your other main goal is to ensure that you always fit 11 GCD's into the buff window. This allows both Goring Blades in this phase to benefit from the damage increase. To accomplish this its extremely important to make sure that your Fight or Flight cast is weaved in the second half of the turn of your GCD as well as that you never clip into your next GCD while weaving your other oGCD's during the buff.

Lower recast times from having higher skillspeeds can make the 11 GCD window less tight but will result in lower overall dps from the loss other substats in exchange for skillspeed. Thus it's important to practice your weave timings as much as possible to find the best recast time for your latency.

Requiescat will come off cooldown later when using the Requiescat first openers. Use it as soon as it is off cooldown as you do other cooldowns.

Requiescat Phase

Starting immediately after your last buffed goring blade you first cast 4 Holy Spirits followed by your Confiteor combo. You should already have the Requiescat buff up from using it during your Fight or Flight phase, but if for some reason you do not you can weave Requiescat between Goring Blade and your first Holy Spirit cast without clipping.

It's very important to pay attention to your Requiescat stacks during this phase. Make sure that you don't lose your Confiteor combo by either using all 5 stacks on other spells or by letting the buff time out. If the boss is going to become untargetable for longer than your buff would last or is about to die it is better to use Confiteor immediately rather than lose a cast of it.

Circle of Scorn and Expiacion should also come off cooldown during this phase and should be weaved as soon as possible. If using a Requiescat opener they will not be up till the Unbuffed phase though. Try not to use Intervene at this time unless you will have 2 charges for an extended time or if the movement is required. Otherwise it is better to save them for the next Fight or Flight phase.

Unbuffed Phase

The shortest of phases, the unbuffed phase simply passes time between the end of your Requiescat phase and the next Fight or Flight phase. With the DoT from Blade of Valor ticking you will skip applying Goring Blade and instead go right into a Royal Authority combo. After using the Atonement stacks generated you will go back to the start of the Fight or Flight phase and begin the loop again.

61s or 64s Loop

While the shortest, the Unbuffed phase is also where you have a choice that will affect the rest of your rotation. You can choose to use all three atonement stacks that you generate here, or you can use only two of them.

Using all three stacks here makes the entire rotation loop take around 64 seconds. This means that Fight or Flight will spend an extra GCD off cooldown before it is pressed as well as slowly misaligning your buff windows with your party's 60s and 120s buffs. Depending on various encounter specific variables this can cause a loss of DPS.

Using only two of your atonement stacks in your Unbuffed phase brings your rotation loop down to around 61 seconds. This gets Fight or Flight used again a GCD sooner and will help keep your buffs inline with your party's at the cost of an unbuffed atonement. This can be a DPS gain depending on the encounter.

Which should you use? Dropping the third Atonement for the 61s loop is generally recommended. That being said, as with all small job optimizations, the difference in DPS between the two options will not be as large of a DPS difference as playing well, maintaining uptime on the boss, and executing the rest of your rotation correctly.
